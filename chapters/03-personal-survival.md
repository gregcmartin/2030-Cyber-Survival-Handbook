---

Personal cybersecurity in 2030 is identity survival.

The bots do not just break into accounts.

They become you.

---

## Reduce Your Online Footprint

Start deleting.

- Close accounts you do not use.
- Remove old posts that reveal family names, schools, employers, birthdays, routines, and travel patterns.
- Delete abandoned blogs, forums, portfolios, and public profiles.
- Opt out of data broker sites where possible.
- Remove personal documents from cloud folders that do not need them.
- Stop using your real birthday for services that do not legally need it.
- Do not publish your phone number unless you are ready for it to become attack infrastructure.

Your past is searchable.

Make it smaller.

---

## Rebuild Your Password Life

If you can remember your important passwords, they are probably too weak or reused.

Use a password manager. Generate unique passwords for every account.

Start with:

- Email
- Bank accounts
- Brokerage accounts
- Payroll
- Cloud storage
- Phone carrier
- Domain registrar
- Password manager itself
- Apple, Google, Microsoft, Amazon, and Meta accounts

Then keep going.

Old reused passwords are one of the cheapest ways to become a victim.

---

## Use Hardware Security Keys

Use hardware keys for critical accounts whenever possible.

Protect:

- Primary email
- Password manager
- Banking and brokerage accounts
- Cloud admin accounts
- Developer accounts
- Domain registrar
- Company identity provider

Keep at least two keys.

One daily key.

One recovery key stored safely offline.

---

## Stop Depending on SMS

Text-message codes became one of the weakest links.

Attackers learned to steal numbers, trick carriers, intercept messages, and manipulate support desks.

Use authenticator apps or hardware keys instead of SMS where possible.

Then protect your phone carrier account with:

- A strong unique password
- A carrier PIN
- Port-out protection
- Account change alerts

---

## Secure Your Devices

Your phone and laptop are the keys to your kingdom.

Do the basics well:

- Keep operating systems updated.
- Use full-disk encryption.
- Use a strong device passcode.
- Remove apps you do not use.
- Disable browser extensions you do not trust.
- Separate work and personal devices when possible.
- Do not install unknown profiles, certificates, remote management tools, or "security helpers."

Convenience software became one of the easiest ways in.

---

## Create a Recovery Binder

Make an offline recovery plan.

Include:

- Emergency contacts
- Bank phone numbers
- Insurance contacts
- Account recovery steps
- Hardware key locations
- Backup locations
- Lawyer or estate contact
- Company incident contacts
- Instructions for freezing credit

Print it.

Store it somewhere safe.

When the network lies, paper is still honest.
