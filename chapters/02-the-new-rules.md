# The New Rules

[Home](../README.md) | [Previous](01-the-leak-year.md) | [Next: Personal Survival](03-personal-survival.md)

---

The old internet rewarded visibility.

The new internet punishes unnecessary exposure.

These are the rules that kept me alive.

---

## 1. Be Boring to Attack

Bots rank targets.

They score people, companies, accounts, vendors, domains, employees, executives, wallets, devices, and cloud systems.

Your goal is not to be impossible to attack.

Your goal is to be expensive, confusing, low-value, and slow.

---

## 2. Delete Before You Defend

Every forgotten account is a loose wire.

Every old password is a buried mine.

Every abandoned profile is a mask someone else can wear.

Security starts by removing what you no longer need.

---

## 3. Trust No Single Point of Failure

One email should not control your life.

One phone number should not unlock your money.

One cloud account should not hold your business.

One person should not be able to approve a company-destroying action.

---

## 4. Slow Down Money

Speed helps attackers.

Add delays, limits, approvals, freezes, alerts, and human verification before money moves.

Urgency is not proof.

It is often the attack.

---

## 5. Separate Public Life From Private Control

Your public identity can be noisy.

Your recovery email, banking phone number, password manager, hardware keys, legal documents, backups, and admin accounts should be quiet.

Do not let the same identity run everything.

---

## 6. Prepare Before the Event

During an attack, you will be tired, rushed, scared, and surrounded by fake signals.

Your plan must already exist.

Paper still matters.

Trusted phone numbers still matter.

People who know the plan still matter.
