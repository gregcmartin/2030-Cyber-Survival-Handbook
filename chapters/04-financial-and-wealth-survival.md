# Financial and Wealth Survival

[Home](../README.md) | [Previous](03-personal-survival.md) | [Next: Company and Corporate Network Survival](05-company-and-corporate-survival.md)

---

The bots learned that identity is the path to money.

They did not need to beat banks directly if they could beat you, your family, your assistant, your phone company, your email, your payroll system, your accountant, or your vendors.

---

## Freeze Your Credit

Freeze your credit with the major credit bureaus.

Keep it frozen unless you are actively applying for credit.

This blocks many attempts to open new accounts in your name.

---

## Reduce Financial Attack Surface

You do not need unlimited financial doors.

Review:

- Credit cards
- Store cards
- Payment apps
- Crypto accounts
- Old bank accounts
- Old brokerage accounts
- Buy-now-pay-later accounts
- Online wallets
- Payroll portals

Close what you do not use.

Lower limits where practical.

Turn on alerts for every transaction.

---

## Be Careful With Credit Cards

Credit cards can provide fraud protection, but too many open cards create more places to monitor and more ways to be abused.

Use a small, intentional setup:

- Keep only the cards you actually use.
- Use virtual card numbers for online merchants when available.
- Set spending alerts.
- Lock cards when not in use if your bank supports it.
- Avoid storing cards in random websites.
- Remove saved payment methods from old services.

Do not let convenience become a quiet financial network you no longer control.

---

## Separate Wealth From Spending

Your spending account should not be your wealth account.

Use separation:

- One account for daily spending
- One account for bills
- One account for savings
- Separate investment custody where appropriate
- No debit card attached to large balances
- Transfer limits where possible

If one account is compromised, the blast radius should be limited.

---

## Require Human Verification for Large Moves

No urgent money movement based only on email, text, chat, video, or voice.

Confirm through a known channel.

Use a pre-agreed phrase with family or business partners for emergencies.

Assume any voice can be cloned.

Assume any video can be fake.

Assume urgency is part of the attack.

---

## Keep Some Offline Resilience

Do not build a life that fails completely when accounts are frozen.

Keep:

- A small cash reserve
- Copies of key documents
- Offline backups of important records
- Multiple ways to contact family
- More than one banking relationship if your situation justifies it

You are not preparing for the end of the world.

You are preparing for a week when the digital world does not recognize you.
