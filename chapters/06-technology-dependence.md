# Technology Dependence and Surface Management

---

The richest targets in 2030 were not always the biggest.

They were the messiest.

Too many apps. Too many integrations. Too many permissions. Too many vendors. Too many old accounts. Too many forgotten automations. Too much trust.

---

## Shrink the Stack

Ask this every quarter:

Do we still need this?

Delete:

- Unused SaaS tools
- Old admin accounts
- Dead domains
- Abandoned cloud projects
- Forgotten API keys
- Unused browser extensions
- Legacy remote access tools
- Old analytics tags
- Duplicate file-sharing systems

Every tool is a contract with risk.

---

## Manage Your Public Surface

Search for yourself and your company the way an attacker would.

Look for:

- Exposed emails
- Employee lists
- Old resumes
- Public calendars
- Leaked credentials
- Public code secrets
- Open storage buckets
- Forgotten subdomains
- Personal phone numbers
- Vendor documentation
- Screenshots showing internal tools

Remove what you can.

Monitor what remains.

---

## Separate Critical Systems From Entertainment

Do not mix everything.

Your gaming account, family tablet, smart TV, crypto wallet, business email, payroll approval, and production admin panel should not live in one sloppy identity cloud.

Create boundaries:

- Personal
- Family
- Financial
- Work
- Administrative
- Recovery

Boundaries turn disasters into contained incidents.

---

## Build Offline Modes

If your life or company cannot function without one cloud provider, one login, one phone number, or one payment rail, you are fragile.

Create fallback options for:

- Communication
- Payments
- Documents
- Customer support
- Payroll
- Vendor contact
- Identity recovery
- Critical operations

The best digital system still needs an analog escape hatch.

---

## Choose Fewer, Better Vendors

Cheap tools became expensive after compromise.

Prefer vendors with:

- Strong security controls
- Good audit logs
- Hardware key support
- Clear account recovery
- Admin role separation
- Data export
- Incident transparency
- Fast support for account takeover

In 2030, vendor choice became a security decision.
