This is not a hacker manual.

This is a survival manual.

By 2030, the internet had stopped being a place where humans attacked humans with software. It became a battlefield of autonomous systems, synthetic identities, cloned voices, fake documents, poisoned feeds, breached vendors, and AI agents fighting other AI agents at machine speed.

The old advice was still true:

- Use strong passwords.
- Do not click suspicious links.
- Install updates.

It was also too small.

After the 2026-2027 tipping point, leaked frontier models and unrestricted local models changed cybercrime from a craft into an industry. Some came from U.S. labs. Some came from Chinese model groups. Some were stolen. Some were open. Some were modified until every safety layer was gone.

The forums called it **abliteration**: strip the refusal behavior, remove the guardrails, attach tools, and let the model operate.

The bots did not need to hate you.

They only needed to find you profitable.

This handbook is about becoming hard to find, hard to impersonate, hard to drain, hard to trap, and hard to ruin.

---

## The Survival Mindset

You are not trying to win a cyber war by yourself.

You are trying to stop being easy.

Delete what you do not need. Separate what must not fail. Slow down money. Protect identity. Keep backups. Build manual recovery paths. Make every attack require more work than you are worth.

That is survival.
